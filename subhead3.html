I love Library

There is no requirement to get into reading books, whether it is physical, digital or audiobooks. Anyone can read, you just need motiviation to get started, reading is fun!

Getting Started

Before you get started on your reading journey, it is important to know where to start picking up books based on their genres. While I can’t list all, the self-explanatory names should give you an idea about the content of the books. Some books contain multiple genres and will appear in each label.

One easy way to guess the type of genre you may like is by referencing based on your type of media you consume, it does not matter if its movies or video games, you can deduce based on themes. Or you can just grab a book because of the premise sounds interesting, the world is your oyster.

Accessing Physical Books

Getting physical books are easy as grabbing it at grocery stores, but your mileage may vary in selections. In big retail stores you are guaranteed to have at least a shelf with multiple genres sorted, or you can go out of your way to visit dedicated bookstores or secondhand stores. And of course you can always grab them at your local public library.

It is hard to tell how much bookstores have changed over the years ever since its popularity on social media, one thing I will always remember is visiting Barnes & Noble back in the day where the manga shelf was only one, if you check any this year, they likely have bigger dedicated section for manga and light novels. You will be paying full price at Barnes & Noble, if you rather save money, your best bet is to find them in any secondhand stores or buy them online. One site I recommend buying physical books is called ThriftBooks, but be aware that depending on the popularity of the books, the retail value versus second hand may not be much different. Another site I’ve seen being recommended is called Pango, it is a secondhand site where you buy off readers looking to sell their books.

I thought it would be important to share the different type of of book printing to help identify even though it is obvious to many.

Before we discuss the simple guide of books you can find in the wild, I will note that I won’t deep dive and explain a lot of the nuances in printed books regardless of which it was published in. But there will be a brief mention of content in books, assuming it applies to either hardcover or softcover/paperback since I can’t definitively check for all types of books.

Hard Cover

Books that contain stiffer boards and a dust jacket, made to last longer than paperback for resistance to wear from repeated use. Also are made with higher quality materials and papers, tend to cost more.

Special Edition Hard Cover

Publishing company may print out limited edition books that are exclusive, usually contain alternative art cover on dust jacket, have simple decorative foil on the cover, and colored spray edges. For example, Barnes & Noble standard exclusive book usually contain bookmark or poster.

Subscription Box Service

Some of the most notable companies are Book of the Month, OwlCrate, FairyLoot, Illumicrate, etc. These tend to ship out books in hard cover and depending on the subscription service, they may tailor to have additional items or have exclusive book version with author note and signature. These types of books are high in collector value and tend to follow the book trend in whichever is popular now, but I find them only worth it if you liked the alternative art that is in collab with these companies and the book.

Paperback

Spray edges are not just for Hardcover, some paperbacks do have it and depending on the edition, it may have its own cover flaps mimicking dust jacket. But did you know that publisher may just release a common paperback but with spray edges? Spray edges just recently became something to drive up sales and for attractive looks. Anyways let’s see the difference in why some paperbacks are sized differently compared to some that are much smaller.

Trade Paperback

Paperback that has higher quality paper and materials, can be about the same size as a standard hardcover.

Mass Market Paperback

A book that is made to be mass produced in a short amount of time and are extremely portable, but due to the low quality of the pages and materials, they do not last as nearly as long.

When I buy second hand, I tend to go for mass market because I do like ths smaller size and other than it being easy to carry around, they possibly can save a lot of space on my bookshelf and are cheap.

I have found an article that goes into detail about the difference between trade paperback and mass market paperback if you are interested. See here.

Audiobooks

Audiobooks is a form of reading where the user listen to an audio format of the book, can be found through Libby/Overdrive with your library card or you can purchase through a subscription service. The most popular option for listening to audiobook is called Audible, owned by Amazon. Book content within the audiobook may have different version depending on the popularity called dramatized. These include sound effects, full voice actor cast, are made to be immersive than the standard audiobook spoken from one voice actor. Audiobook is an accessible format for people who has visual disability which is a neat inclusive way to reading. It also be used in other ways such as hands free for other tasks or for easy on the go option from your phone or car.

Extra

Some debate on if listening to an audiobook is considered a form of “reading”, it can be a little confusing since when you address that you “read” a book via listening from audiobook, you say I read this book rather than I listened to this book.

Accessing Digital Books

You have many options to view digital books or E-Books, the most common form of reading E-Books is through your phone or through a E-Reader device, computer is fine. You can buy E-Books through Amazon which is the most accessible or through other services found on the internet. A low-cost alternative is renting from your library, it will allow you access to their digital database of leased E-Books. From what I know, the current app is called Libby which replaced the older Overdrive app, but Overdrive can still be accessed through your library Overdrive link. Libby does provide audiobooks so does Overdrive, renting from either is the same as renting physical books, but this time you are allowed to adjust the loan time.

Amazon is the only company that provide ease of use when you use their services, having an Amazon account allows you to sync your digital books purchased or rented to your Kindle as long as it is connected to the internet.

Extra

There is a hot debate on when purchasing E-Books for the sole reason of owning the digital file, apparently you don’t because Amazon purchased the license to lease the digital books on the storefront. And obviously to use the digital content from them, you must use their services, and the digital contents are protected by DRM which is a type of copyright that protects the Intellectual Property. Not everyone like this because you simply cannot “own” a digital property that you spent money on. Another reason is due to corporation having the power to restrict you from having the digital content because you are on their turf, they are allowed to get rid of digital content they hosted out of anyone who has the lease without informing. The only way to get around this is to buy physical copies.

Kindle is on a similar vein because the device is made for Amazon services and can only be used if there is an active Amazon account. There is no other way around it.

E-Reader

This will mainly cover E-Reader devices, this section will just go over some alternative options of devices that are like Kindle. Keep in mind that most options listed are made to be distraction free as in it does not do too many things outside of the OS, just made for reading. I may in the future cover the ‘reading tablet’ within some brands, but I will mainly be talking about E-Readers that use E-Ink technology. Lastly, if you ever consider getting any of the E-Readers, research your needs.

Kindle

Produced and manufactured by Amazon LLC., is one of the most popular E-Reader in this market for its vast database, released since the late 2000’s.

If you are not techie like me, the Kindle Basic is your best option if you don’t see yourself picking it up frequently and, on a budget, it does what you need in a typical E-Reader, reading your E-Book. Kindle Paperwhite have some quality-of-life upgrades such as warm light, faster processing, bigger screen, flush screen and wireless charging present in Signature Edition.

While newer Kindle generation gets Usb-C, only in the 10th generation (2018) were stuck with micro usb port despite the universal use of Usb-C.

Ever since the new Kindle refresh, you can manually update any older Kindle device to the current OS version that would match the fast speed present in the newest model.

Kobo

Has multiple variety of E-Reader devices and recently created a colored E-Ink device in their lineup, Kobo do have their own active E-Book store to purchase from with their device.

Nook

Barnes & Noble E-Reader, currently only have one E-Reader, both Glowlight 4 and Glowlight 4 Plus are available on their site. If you were tripping out and swore, they used to sell the Nook back in the day, you are right. Nook discontinued their E-Book and digital service on their Bookstore of this year, unfortunate.

LCD/OLED Tablet

Basically, I’ve seen people use their iPad to “convert” it into an E-Reader by using the Kindle App. It is doable to do, but if bright colorful screen hurts your eye that’s where E-Ink comes in. And if having an option to switch app distracts you from reading, that’s where a dedicated E-Reader comes in.

There is Reading Tablets that functions like a standard tablet but has the company E-Book store attached, but they will be written up soon.

Extra

Companies in this market are now capitalizing on the color E-Ink, I personally prefer the standard black and white. I can only see a use in color if you want colored highlights or see colored graphic novels and maybe magazines. Other than that, don’t expect the colors to be very vibrant like an OLED screen *cough* ipad *cough*, they are in their infancy stage. Overall E-Reader is a cool product for a niche hobby especially the E-Ink technology it is fitting.
